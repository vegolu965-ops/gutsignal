Why This Works — When Nothing Else Has

Most gut health tools look at symptoms in isolation. We look at why your stomach is struggling — by mapping the three systems that control it.

🫁

The Liver-Gut Loop

Your liver filters everything absorbed from your gut. When this pathway congests — from diet patterns, stress, or medications — it sends a "slow down" signal to your stomach. Less acid. Slower digestion. Reflux that antacids can't fix.

🧠

The Nerve-Gut Axis

Your stomach needs a clear signal from the vagus nerve to function. Chronic stress overrides this signal — food sits undigested, bloating builds, and your gut clock desynchronizes. The fix isn't another pill. It's restoring the signal.

🩸

The Blood-Mucosa Connection

Your stomach lining renews itself every 3–5 days — one of the fastest-healing tissues in your body. But renewal requires delivery: amino acids, minerals, oxygen, all carried by blood. When circulation is weak, the repair crew never gets the materials.

🕐

The 28-Day Cycle

Your stomach lining rebuilds in 21–28 days. Your liver cells regenerate in ~21 days. Your nervous system resets its rhythm in ~28 days. We didn't choose 28 days — your biology did. It's the minimum window for all three systems to complete one full repair cycle.
